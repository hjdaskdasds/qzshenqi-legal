Why this is worth validating

8.9score
Source evidence

A freelance web designer asked a small-business community how to handle a substantially overdue invoice and statutory late-payment interest. The discussion makes the buyer, owed payment, and uncertainty visible.

Product hypothesis

A lightweight assistant that creates staged reminder emails, records promises to pay, schedules follow-ups, and explains which facts the sender should confirm before escalating.

MVP scope

Start as a guided form: invoice amount, due date, buyer type, prior messages, desired tone, and escalation preference. Output three reminder drafts and a follow-up calendar.

Pricing hypothesis

Test $9-15 per month for repeat freelancers or a $19 one-off recovery pack for a single overdue invoice workflow.

Validation test

Offer a manual review to ten freelancers: turn one overdue invoice into a three-step follow-up sequence and ask whether they would pay for future reminders and tracking.

Primary risk

Late-payment rules differ by jurisdiction. Position the product as workflow and communication support, not legal advice or guaranteed collection.

Primary source: Reddit r/smallbusinessuk, Statutory Late Payment Interest - Advice. Reviewed July 13, 2026.

Search intent this page can capture

People searching for overdue invoice reminder software, freelancer invoice follow-up templates, polite payment reminder emails, or late-payment workflow tools are already near the problem. A useful product page should lead with the concrete reminder sequence, then add tracking only after the user trusts the workflow.

48-hour validation plan

  1. Write one practical guide. Explain the first, second, and final overdue-invoice reminder without promising a legal outcome.
  2. Offer a manual pack. Convert one invoice into email drafts, a follow-up schedule, and a payment-status tracker.
  3. Measure willingness to pay. Ask users to buy the next pack or subscribe only after they see the first result.
Research limitation: A public Reddit discussion proves that this problem exists for the people involved. It does not prove market size, conversion rate, or legal suitability across countries.
